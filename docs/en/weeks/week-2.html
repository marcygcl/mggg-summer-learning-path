Week 2

Electoral systems

4 min read · Status: Done

Where I was starting from

With the census basics down, I turned to the systems themselves — the rules that take a pile of ballots and decide who actually wins.

What I explored

Plurality: most votes wins, no majority required. IRV (instant-runoff): voters rank, the lowest candidate is eliminated and their ballots redistribute to next preferences, repeating until someone holds a majority. STV (single transferable vote): IRV's idea for multi-winner seats, using a Droop quota to produce roughly proportional outcomes.

I worked a three-candidate IRV example by hand, and a Droop-quota example for STV, just to feel the mechanics rather than trust the words.

The moment it clicked

Aha

Electoral systems encode values. Plurality favours the largest bloc; IRV forces broader consensus; STV produces proportional representation. Choosing a system is a political choice, not a neutral technicality.
